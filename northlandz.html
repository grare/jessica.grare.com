NORTHLANDZ HAS WORLD'S LARGEST MODEL RAILROAD

They won a Guinness World Record for that feat. However, many family attractions, such as the Morris Museum, are taking away Northlandz's customers. The competitors' websites are easy to navigate. It's time to modernize the Northlandz website and make it a leading competitor in the party space.

To show the potential of what Northlandz can offer, I revitalized key website pages: the home and events webpages. As an event planner, I proposed party packages that make parents excited about booking a party at Northlandz.

Home Page

A First Impression That Earns the Visit

The current site is cluttered with dated graphics, cluttered navigation, and too much copy. Its redesign is inspired by museum and children's party venue website. The redesigned home page leads with the railroad and exhibits, makes the booking process straightforward, and gives families a clear reason to visit.

Events Page

A Party Booking Experience Built for Parents

The original events page included outdated stock images, inauthentic testimonials, lots of copy, and vague information about the parties themselves. This event dedicates itself to Northlandz's main clientele: families throwing parties for their children. There is a basic description to get parents hooked, followed up a simple run-down of the different party packages offered. Competitors have event designers on retainer, creating themes exclusively for their venues. Northlandz will follow suit and offer themes that compliment the museum. Parents can choose between the "Steam Train" theme to match the train exhibit or the "Vintage Princess Magic" theme to match the vintage doll collection. Photos of the themes in their space gives parents assurance of the party's execution. The multiple buttons and testimonials are replaced with a simple contact form.

Party Packages

For Northlandz to compete in the party space, they need to break down what type of party they can offer and what is included. Packages can begin with a pass to the museum, and clients can add tickets to their ride-on train or make a craft from the gift shop's inventory. Northlandz can also use their gift shop inventory put together the perfect favor bags. Food and desserts from local businesses would be provided. It is important for them to emphasize that they support small businesses from Flemington, NJ.

Getting On Track

Top-notch competitors have an event designer on retainer. Northlandz could provide packages exclusive to their location and theme, catering to both boys and girls. One theme could be a homage to their outdoor train. The other could be a vintage princess theme, referencing their antique doll collection.
