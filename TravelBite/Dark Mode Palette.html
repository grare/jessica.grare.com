Drop-in CSS

straight from styles.css
[data-theme="dark"] {
  --paper:        #1c1610;
  --paper-2:      #251c14;
  --paper-3:      #2f2419;
  --ink:          #f0e3cc;
  --ink-2:        #c9b691;
  --ink-3:        #d0b591;
  --rule:         #4a3a26;
  --terracotta:   #e07a5a;
  --terracotta-2: #c25a3b;
  --turmeric:     #e3b34a;
  --saffron:      #f0c970;
  --sage:         #8a9c79;
  --plum:         #c889a4;
  --stamp-red:    #e07a6a;
  --stamp-blue:   #6b9bc9;
  --shadow-paper: 0 1px 0 rgba(255,255,255,0.04) inset, 0 8px 24px rgba(0,0,0,0.4);
}

Rules of thumb

  • Ink-3 lifts from #8B7253 in light mode to #D0B591 in dark — eyebrows must stay readable on coffee paper.
  • Never use pure white for text. Top out at #F0E3CC — pure white reads cold against the warm browns.
  • Never use pure black for surfaces. Bottom out at #1C1610 — keeps the leather/passport feel.
  • Stamps shift to the brightened red #E07A6A and blue #6B9BC9; rotation and dashed inner ring stay the same.
  • Spice discs use the brightened dark accents (e.g. plum becomes #C889A4) so glyphs stay readable. The disc-glyph color flips to #1C1610 on light-saturation discs.
  • Saffron (#F0C970) is the brightest accent — reserve it for streak counts and lit numerals, not large surfaces.
  • Shadows shift from warm brown rgba(80,50,20,.12) to deep rgba(0,0,0,.40). Layer is what reads, not lift.
  • Caveat script stays terracotta — it's the hand-written warmth, and it pops harder on dark paper.
