Helping Children "Bear" the Itch

Alagille syndrome is the buildup of bile acids in the liver and blood because the bile ducts cannot allow bile acids to flow out of the liver. The buildup of bile acids in the liver and blood can cause an intense itch. To demonstrate how itching can impact a child, the child was represented as a bear with diseased skin itching himself down to the stuffing. The campaign was called "The Unbearable Itch."

I was on a client team working on Art Direction for an activity book telling children the story of how the bear sought help for Alagille syndrome and got better. I worked on the book layout, collaborated with the design company DEEP for illustrations, and designed the activities for the children.

Pediatric patients had a story to read, activities to enjoy, and a log of how they are feeling each week.
