An Homage to Home Cooks with Wanderlust

TravelBite is a recipe app that teaches curious cooks how to cook international dishes from inside and beyond their pantry. Traditional recipe apps are based on logging search results and creating a shopping list. TravelBite’s foundation is having the recipes be a logbook, not a search bar. The question, “What shall we cook?” transforms into “Where shall we cook?” Every meal served turns into a stamp on a personal food passport, having users log their culinary journey around the world, from the comforts of their kitchen.

The Concept

Most recipe apps treat cooking like a transaction — search, scroll, shop. TravelBite reframes it as a journey. Every dish becomes a destination, every meal a stamp on a personal food passport. The brand mark draws from vintage travel postage and well-worn cookbooks: warm, tactile, and a little bit handmade.

Logo Design

Passport meets pantry is the goal of the illustration, signaling both travel and home cooking. Earth tones keep it grounded and warm.

Brand Typography

4 families · 10 roles

Four typefaces carry the entire app, each doing one job. DM Serif Display is the headline voice — warm, slightly bookish, used for dish names and screen titles. Inter handles all running UI text and body copy. JetBrains Mono is the “stamped” voice for eyebrows and metadata, with the authority of a passport entry. Caveat is the script note — used sparingly to give a recipe or postcard the hand-written feel of a travel journal.
