It All Started with an Orange Sticky Note.

According to the American Foundation for Suicide Prevention (AFSP), a 2023 study showed that suicide is the 11th leading cause of death in the United States. McCann Health created a call to action for suicide prevention via The Orange Project (#worthliving). Words of inspiration and positivity were written on orange sticky notes to demonstrate how life is worth living.

As an art director on the campaign, I designed email campaigns, social media filters, agency promotions, and a physical mock yearbook summarizing The Orange Project. I helped photograph over 34,244 handwritten orange sticky notes that were placed into an online photo album for millions to view. The photo album won a Guinness World Record for the largest online photo album. The yearbook won the Rx Club Award of Excellence in November 2018.

McCann Health represented the Orange Project by attending suicide walks, the NYC Pride Parade, and a Penn State basketball game. I wore posterboards with the notes at these events for attendees to write on them.

Taylor's Yearbook / The Orange Project

A school yearbook commemorates accomplishments in school and celebrates life beyond high school. To raise sucide awareness and reduce its stigma, a yearbook was designed to depict a teenager lost to suicide and how her death affected the school. When people band together to speak up, stigma—and yearbook—is flipped upside down.
